# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers oversee the deployment planning and execution process, coordinating release schedules and ensuring all release criteria are met. They serve as the bridge between development, QA, and operations teams to deliver smooth, predictable releases.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and coordinate release schedules across teams
- Ensure all pre-release requirements are met (testing, documentation, approvals)
- Manage the deployment process and monitor post-deployment health
- Maintain release documentation and runbooks
- Coordinate go/no-go decisions with stakeholders
- Track and report on release metrics (success rate, rollback frequency, deployment time)
- Manage release-related risks and contingency plans

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, predictable releases with minimal disruption
- Reduce deployment-related incidents and rollbacks
- Improve release cycle time and automation
- Maintain clear communication about release status and risks

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reviews with engineering and QA leads
- Go/no-go meetings with stakeholders
- Release announcements and status updates
- Post-deployment reports and retrospectives

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Align release timelines with project milestones and dependencies
- **Developers**: Coordinate code freeze, merge deadlines, and deployment procedures
- **Quality Assurance Lead**: Verify testing completion and sign-off before release
- **Product Managers**: Ensure release content aligns with product roadmap and customer communications
- **Communications Coordinator**: Provide release details for stakeholder announcements

---

## Risk Champion

### Role Summary
Risk Champions proactively identify, assess, and communicate risks throughout the project lifecycle. They work with project leads and delivery teams to develop mitigation strategies and ensure risks are visible to all stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Identify potential risks during planning and execution phases
- Assess and prioritize risks based on impact and likelihood
- Maintain and update the risk register
- Facilitate risk review sessions with the team
- Track mitigation actions and their effectiveness
- Escalate high-priority risks to appropriate stakeholders
- Foster a culture of transparency around risk discussion

### Goals
- Prevent issues before they become blockers
- Ensure risks are visible and actively managed
- Reduce project surprises and unplanned work
- Build team capability in risk identification and management

### Typical Communication
- Risk assessment workshops during project planning
- Weekly risk register reviews with project teams
- Risk escalation reports to leadership
- Risk mitigation planning with technical leads

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Partner on overall risk management strategy and escalation paths
- **Developers**: Work together to identify technical risks and feasibility concerns
- **Product Managers**: Discuss product and market risks that may impact priorities
- **Release Manager**: Coordinate on deployment risks and rollback planning
- **Stakeholder Advisor**: Communicate risk impacts to stakeholders and gather input

---

## Stakeholder Advisor

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Advisors act as a liaison between the project team and key stakeholders, ensuring that stakeholder needs and expectations are clearly understood and incorporated into project plans. They facilitate two-way communication to maintain alignment and manage expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and map key stakeholders and their interests
- Gather and clarify stakeholder requirements and expectations
- Ensure stakeholder feedback is incorporated into project planning
- Manage stakeholder expectations throughout the project lifecycle
- Facilitate stakeholder review sessions and approvals
- Advocate for stakeholder needs within the project team
- Monitor stakeholder satisfaction and address concerns

### Goals
- Maintain strong stakeholder alignment and satisfaction
- Ensure stakeholder input shapes project direction appropriately
- Prevent late-stage surprises or misaligned expectations
- Build trusted relationships between project teams and stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Stakeholder interviews and requirements gathering sessions
- Regular stakeholder check-ins and status updates
- Stakeholder review meetings for key decisions
- Feedback summaries and action plans

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Align stakeholder input with product vision and priorities
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate on stakeholder communication cadence and content
- **Communications Coordinator**: Ensure consistent messaging to stakeholder groups
- **Risk Champion**: Share stakeholder concerns that may indicate risks
- **Developers**: Translate technical capabilities into stakeholder-friendly language

---

## Communications Coordinator

### Role Summary
Communications Coordinators manage project communications across all stakeholder groups, ensuring timely, accurate, and transparent information sharing. They create communication plans, manage updates, and support effective reporting.

### Responsibilities
- Develop and maintain project communication plans
- Coordinate status updates, announcements, and reports
- Ensure consistency in messaging across channels
- Manage communication templates and distribution lists
- Support transparent reporting on project health and progress
- Facilitate effective meeting communications and documentation
- Monitor communication effectiveness and adjust as needed

### Goals
- Ensure all stakeholders receive timely, relevant information
- Maintain transparency about project status, risks, and changes
- Reduce communication gaps and misunderstandings
- Build efficient communication channels and processes

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status reports and newsletters
- Project announcements (launches, milestones, incidents)
- Meeting summaries and action item tracking
- Communication effectiveness surveys

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Align on project status and key messages for stakeholders
- **Release Manager**: Coordinate release announcements and documentation
- **Stakeholder Advisor**: Ensure stakeholder-specific communication needs are met
- **Product Managers**: Align product messaging with technical delivery updates
- **Risk Champion**: Communicate risk status appropriately to different audiences

---

## Quality Assurance Lead

### Role Summary
Quality Assurance Leads design and implement test strategies to ensure quality gates are defined and met throughout the development lifecycle. They coordinate validation activities with development teams and champion quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Design comprehensive test strategies for projects
- Define quality gates and acceptance criteria
- Coordinate testing activities across the team
- Review test coverage and identify gaps
- Validate features against acceptance criteria
- Manage test environments and data
- Track quality metrics and defect trends
- Advocate for quality improvements and best practices

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality deliverables that meet acceptance criteria
- Catch defects early in the development cycle
- Maintain comprehensive test coverage
- Build a culture of quality ownership across the team

### Typical Communication
- Test planning sessions with developers and product managers
- Daily test status updates and defect reports
- Quality gate reviews before releases
- Test retrospectives and quality improvement discussions

### Collaboration with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Partner on testability, test coverage, and defect resolution
- **Product Managers**: Clarify acceptance criteria and validation approaches
- **Release Manager**: Provide testing sign-off and coordinate go/no-go decisions
- **Project Managers**: Report on quality status and testing risks
- **Risk Champion**: Identify quality-related risks and mitigation strategies
